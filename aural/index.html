Guide

The craft of spatial sound

8D audio is an illusion of space. By moving a sound source around a virtual head - using the same head-related filtering your ears rely on in the real world - a flat stereo track begins to feel like it exists in the room with you.

  1. I

    Wear headphones

    The spatial illusion is built on binaural cues - subtle timing and tone differences between your two ears. Speakers blur them; headphones preserve them perfectly.

  2. II

    Import a track

    Drop any audio file into the studio. Decoding happens locally in your browser - your music never leaves your device. Vocals and melodic instruments respond most vividly to motion.

  3. III

    Shape the motion

    Choose a path and let the sound travel on its own, or switch to manual and place it precisely - behind your shoulder, high above, close to one ear. Distance and elevation refine the feeling of space.

  4. IV

    Read the globe

    The sphere of points around the listener is a map of space itself. The constellation glows brightest exactly where the sound currently lives, and the golden orb traces its journey.

  5. V

    Export your render

    When the motion feels right, render the entire track offline at full quality. The result is a standard stereo WAV that carries the full 8D effect on any player, anywhere.
